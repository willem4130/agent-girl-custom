# Psychological Triggers for Copywriting

Evidence-based persuasion techniques calibrated by intensity level.

## Low Intensity (Use Freely)

### 1. Curiosity Gaps
Create an information gap the reader needs to close.

**Techniques:**
- "The one thing most brands miss..."
- "What [experts] know that you don't..."
- "The surprising reason why..."
- Open loops that demand resolution

**Example:**
```
"There's a 3-word phrase that turns cold prospects into buyers. Most marketers use its opposite."
```

### 2. Specificity
Specific claims are more believable than vague ones.

**Techniques:**
- Use exact numbers (not "many" but "7,432")
- Include precise timeframes ("in 47 days" not "in weeks")
- Name specific examples
- Quote exact results

**Example:**
```
Generic: "Increase your conversions significantly"
Specific: "Increase conversions by 23.7% in your first 30 days"
```

### 3. Social Proof
People follow the actions of others.

**Techniques:**
- Customer counts ("Join 12,847 others...")
- Testimonials with names and photos
- Case studies with specific results
- Industry recognition and awards
- "As seen in..." logos

**Example:**
```
"Trusted by 47,000+ marketers at companies like Spotify, Airbnb, and Stripe"
```

### 4. Reciprocity
Give value first, ask second.

**Techniques:**
- Lead magnets and free resources
- Helpful content before the pitch
- Free trials and samples
- Unexpected bonuses

**Example:**
```
"Here's a free template worth $97. No email required."
[Then later offer premium product]
```

## Medium Intensity (Use Strategically)

### 5. Authority
Established expertise increases trust.

**Techniques:**
- Credentials and qualifications
- "According to research from [source]..."
- Expert endorsements
- Published work and media features
- Years of experience with proof

**Example:**
```
"Based on Stanford research and tested across 200+ companies"
```

### 6. Commitment & Consistency
People want to stay consistent with past actions.

**Techniques:**
- Start with small yeses
- Remind of previous commitments
- Public declarations
- Progress indicators

**Example:**
```
"You've already invested 3 months learning marketing. Let's make sure that time pays off."
```

### 7. Scarcity
Limited availability increases perceived value.

**Techniques:**
- Limited quantity ("Only 3 spots left")
- Limited time ("Offer ends Friday")
- Exclusive access ("Members only")
- One-time opportunities

**Example:**
```
"We only take 5 new clients per month to maintain quality. 2 spots remaining for January."
```

**Warning:** Only use genuine scarcity. Fake scarcity damages trust.

### 8. Liking
People buy from those they like.

**Techniques:**
- Show personality and humor
- Find common ground
- Be genuinely helpful
- Use conversational tone
- Share relatable stories

**Example:**
```
"I made every mistake in the book so you don't have to."
```

## High Intensity (Use Sparingly)

### 9. Fear of Missing Out (FOMO)
Fear of exclusion or lost opportunity.

**Techniques:**
- Show what others are gaining
- Highlight opportunity cost
- Time-limited benefits
- Exclusive community access

**Example:**
```
"While you're thinking about it, your competitors are implementing."
```

**Warning:** Only use for genuine opportunities. Manufactured FOMO backfires.

### 10. Identity Appeal
People act consistently with their self-image.

**Techniques:**
- "For people who..."
- Aspirational identity labels
- Tribe/community belonging
- Values alignment

**Example:**
```
"This isn't for everyone. It's for the 1% who refuse to settle for average."
```

**Warning:** Can feel manipulative if forced. Identity must genuinely fit.

### 11. Loss Aversion
People feel losses 2x more than equivalent gains.

**Techniques:**
- Frame as loss prevention
- Highlight what they're losing daily
- Show missed opportunities
- Risk of staying the same

**Example:**
```
"Every day without a system costs you 2-3 hours of productive time."
```

### 12. Urgency
Time pressure drives action.

**Techniques:**
- Real deadlines (price increases, enrollment closes)
- Event-driven urgency (seasonal, limited)
- Countdown timers (when genuine)

**Example:**
```
"Early bird pricing ends at midnight. Price goes up $200 tomorrow."
```

**Warning:** Only use real urgency. Fake countdown timers destroy trust.

## Trigger Combinations

### High-Converting Combinations:

1. **Curiosity + Specificity**
   - "The exact 7-step process that generated $247,000 in 90 days"

2. **Social Proof + Authority**
   - "Used by Netflix's growth team, based on Harvard research"

3. **Scarcity + FOMO**
   - "Last cohort sold out in 6 hours. Early access opens tomorrow."

4. **Identity + Loss Aversion**
   - "Top performers don't leave money on the table. Are you?"

## Ethical Guidelines

### DO:
- Use triggers that highlight genuine value
- Be truthful about scarcity and urgency
- Match trigger intensity to relationship stage
- Respect reader intelligence

### DON'T:
- Manufacture fake urgency
- Create artificial scarcity
- Manipulate through fear
- Use triggers that don't fit the context
- Over-stack high-intensity triggers
