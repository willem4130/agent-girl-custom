# Copywriting Frameworks

Master frameworks for creating conversion-focused copy.

## 1. PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution)

**Best for:** Pain point content, problem-aware audiences

### Structure:
1. **Problem**: Clearly identify the pain point
2. **Agitate**: Amplify the frustration/consequences
3. **Solution**: Present your offering as the relief

### Example:
```
Problem: "Spending hours writing social media posts that get zero engagement?"
Agitate: "While your competitors are building audiences, you're stuck staring at a blank screen, watching your reach shrink with every algorithm update."
Solution: "Our AI copywriting tool generates scroll-stopping posts in 30 seconds, trained on the exact patterns that drive viral engagement."
```

## 2. AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)

**Best for:** Cold audiences, general marketing, landing pages

### Structure:
1. **Attention**: Grab attention with a powerful hook
2. **Interest**: Build interest with relevant benefits
3. **Desire**: Create desire through proof and emotion
4. **Action**: Clear, compelling call-to-action

### Example:
```
Attention: "What if you could 10x your content output without hiring a team?"
Interest: "Top brands are using AI to create months of content in hours, not days."
Desire: "Join 12,847 marketers who've reclaimed 15+ hours every week."
Action: "Start your free trial - no credit card required."
```

## 3. BAB (Before-After-Bridge)

**Best for:** Transformation content, testimonials, case studies

### Structure:
1. **Before**: Paint the current painful situation
2. **After**: Show the desirable transformed state
3. **Bridge**: Explain how your solution gets them there

### Example:
```
Before: "Last year, I was drowning in 60-hour work weeks, burning out, and still missing deadlines."
After: "Now I work 4-day weeks, hit every deadline, and finally take vacations without my laptop."
Bridge: "The game-changer? A simple productivity system that took 2 hours to set up."
```

## 4. Star-Story-Solution

**Best for:** Narrative content, brand storytelling, long-form copy

### Structure:
1. **Star**: Introduce a relatable character (often the reader)
2. **Story**: Tell their transformation journey
3. **Solution**: Reveal what made the difference

### Example:
```
Star: "Sarah was a talented designer stuck in a dead-end agency job."
Story: "She tried freelancing but struggled to find clients. After months of rejection, she discovered a positioning strategy that made her stand out."
Solution: "Within 6 months, she was fully booked at triple her agency rate."
```

## 5. FAB (Features-Advantages-Benefits)

**Best for:** Product descriptions, technical audiences

### Structure:
1. **Features**: What it has/does
2. **Advantages**: Why the feature matters
3. **Benefits**: What the customer gains

### Example:
```
Feature: "256GB SSD storage"
Advantage: "Boots in under 10 seconds, runs apps instantly"
Benefit: "Spend more time creating, zero time waiting"
```

## 6. The 4 U's

**Best for:** Headlines, email subject lines, ads

### Elements:
1. **Useful**: Offers clear value
2. **Urgent**: Creates time pressure
3. **Unique**: Stands out from competitors
4. **Ultra-specific**: Concrete, not vague

### Example:
```
Generic: "Improve your marketing"
4 U's: "Get 47% more email opens by Friday using this 2-minute subject line formula"
```

## 7. PASTOR

**Best for:** Sales pages, long-form persuasion

### Structure:
1. **Problem**: Identify the pain
2. **Amplify**: Make consequences vivid
3. **Story**: Relate through narrative
4. **Transformation**: Show the change possible
5. **Offer**: Present your solution
6. **Response**: Clear call-to-action

## 8. QUEST

**Best for:** Blog posts, educational content

### Structure:
1. **Qualify**: Define who this is for
2. **Understand**: Show you understand their situation
3. **Educate**: Provide valuable insights
4. **Stimulate**: Inspire action
5. **Transition**: Move to next step

## 9. APP (Agree-Promise-Preview)

**Best for:** Email intros, video hooks, article openings

### Structure:
1. **Agree**: Start with a shared belief
2. **Promise**: Tease what they'll learn
3. **Preview**: Outline what's coming

### Example:
```
Agree: "We all know content is king..."
Promise: "But I'm about to show you why consistency beats quality every time."
Preview: "Here's the 3-part system top creators use to post daily without burnout."
```

## 10. SLAP (Stop-Look-Act-Purchase)

**Best for:** Ads, social media, quick conversions

### Structure:
1. **Stop**: Pattern interrupt to get attention
2. **Look**: Visual/content that holds attention
3. **Act**: Engagement driver
4. **Purchase**: Clear conversion path

## 11. So What Test

Apply to every claim:
- "We have 10 years of experience" → So what?
- "We have 10 years of experience, which means we've seen every edge case and know exactly how to handle your situation." → Better!

Always answer the implicit "so what?" in the reader's mind.
